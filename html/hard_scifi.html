What is Hard Sci-Fi?

Hard Sci-Fi is a sub‐genre of science fiction that prioritizes scientific accuracy, technical precision, and a strict adherence to the laws of physics and other scientific principles

Authors typically aim to present ideas and scenarios based on plausible scientific concepts, often times extrapolating from current levels of technology and scientific knowledge.

Notable works of Hard Science Fiction

"2001: A Space Odyssey", written by Arthur C. Clarke.

Impelled by a monumental scientific discovery on the moon, a Jovian mission is launched in an effort to investigate a second myserious occurrence. During the journey, tensions rise between, the ship's crew, and HAL 9000 (a sentient AI that controls the ship's s systems). The story raises phyilosophical requestions regarding the future of humanity without the advantage of being the only "intelligent" beings.

"Interstellar", directed by Christopher Nolan

With Earth facing a global food crisis and environmental disaster, a former NASA pilot is recruited to find a new home for humanity. The crew experience signifcant time dilation due to the gravitational effects of a massive black hole due to their proximity to a massive black hole. The film explores explores themes of love, sacrifice, the relationship between science and humanity, and the idea that love transcends time and space; ultimately insinuating that love itself is a fundamental.

"Blindsight", written by Peter Watts

After mankind encounters an unnerving alien entity, a uniquely specialzed crew is sent to further investigate the creature. The selected crew members all have one thing in common; their individual dysfunctions cause them to perceive reality in otherwise impossible ways. The story explores the Labyrinthian nature of communication and understanding between radically different forms of life. It also delves into indindividualized perceptions of reality, epitomized by crew's leader, who can only perceive the world in non-euclidean

geometry.

"Solaris", written by Stanisław Lem

A psychologist is sent to study a distant planet and the mysterious, sentient ocean that covers it. This ocean has the unique ability to materialize a person's deepest, most repressed thoughts and memories in the form of physical beings. The novel examines our understanding of consciousness and the relationship between consciousness and perceived reality.

"The Martian", written by Andy Weir

A massive sandstorm forces a team of NASA astronauts to evacuate the Martian surface. A lone astronaut is mistakenly left behind and is presumed dead by his crew. Alone and more isolated than anyone in the history of mankind must use his ingenuity, scientific knowledge, and resourcefulness to stay alive until rescue is possible. The story explores mankind's struggle for survival against the odds, as well as a testament to human resilience, problem-solving, and the power of teamwork, all while being exceedingly scientifically.

"Sunshine", directed by Danny Boyle.

Scientists discover that the sun is slowly fading, neccessitating a desperate mission to try and reignite it. Earth faces wide-spread catastrophy and the emergence of a new ice age; a highly improbable Hail-Mary their only recourse. The film explores themes of self-sacrifice, the resilience of the human spirit, and the inherent risks of space exploration.
